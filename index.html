Article 13A — Now Resolved

The Audit Committee rule question, answered directly by the Chair at the 28 March 2026 AGM
Resolved
Which text governs — the 8 March original, or the 19 March amendment?
The original (8 March 2024) text. The Chair confirmed directly: "as per the approved AGM minutes, only the original resolution had been adopted."
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.7
Resolved
What does that confirmed text actually require?
3 members. Chairman must be a Chartered Accountant. Other members should have a finance background. Quorum for meetings is 3.
Original Resolution, 8 Mar 2024; restated at AGM p.7
Flagged — unresolved
Is the current AC Chairman actually a Chartered Accountant?
No. Current AC Chairman Shanker Somasundaram is CIMA-qualified, not a Chartered Accountant — a live breach of the Club's own just-confirmed rule, raised on the floor by the Chair himself.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.7
Flagged — unresolved
Has the Audit Committee met its 3-member quorum requirement?
Disputed. Concern raised that some AC meetings may have lacked quorum. Somasundaram responded all 3 members participated, some virtually — leaving open whether virtual attendance satisfies the rule as written.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.7
Resolved
Is the AC now Trustee-based, or a standalone 3-person elected body?
Standalone 3-person body, matching the confirmed original text — and matching what was described from direct knowledge, now corroborated by the AGM record.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.7

The 2026 Nominations Committee Saga

Nominations Committee: Shanker Somasundaram (Chairman) · Pradeep Cheruvari · Gamini Gangabadaarachchi
13 MAR
Nominations opened. List promised on the notice board by 18 March.
17 MAR
NC sends its findings — the Nomination List, with rejections marked — to the GC.
18 MAR
List still not displayed. Shamalie Fonseka asks for confirmation and verification of the proposer/seconder/CV rules. Somasundaram replies that "the Audit committee" (sic) had handed the info to the GC.
19 MAR
Tilak writes to the NC Chairman: the GC can't fairly judge its own eligibility — if it can override the NC, the NC's role "is rendered meaningless." Separately, Gamini Gangabadaarachchi (an NC member himself) formally warns overriding the NC "would not be ethically appropriate." A separate complaint is filed alleging Kishin Butani ran a WhatsApp group circulating defamatory information about nominees.
23 MAR
Shamalie Fonseka formally requests the final NC list with rejection reasons, election guidelines, and independent observers.
28 MAR
AGM: GC's override confirmed on record — CV submission ruled "not a constitutional requirement." Gamini Gangabadaarachchi states on record this "reflected poorly on the work of the Nominations Sub-Committee."
Round 1 — form/technical
Caught: Tilak Conrad (blank position field, accepted on the spot) and incumbent VP Don Lalith Hilary Ganlath (rejected in his absence — unanimous NC decision). Neither reached Round 2.
Round 2 — substantive (dues, CVs, standing)
Caught: the entire incumbent GC except A. Rajenthiran (via the CV bylaw the GC itself had passed), plus Additional Trustee nominees Shirendra Lawrence and Lucille Wijewardena ("No CV").
Outcome
GC overrode the NC and reinstated the rejected nominees. President uncontested. But the incumbent VP and GenSec still lost anyway — to Mohan Wijesinghe and Shamalie Fonseka. The two reinstated AT nominees (Lawrence, Wijewardena) did win. Kishin Butani — the AT nominee the NC had cleanly approved — did not get elected.

28 March 2026 AGM — The Seven Resolutions

All member-proposed, all passed
1 · Conflict of interest — family members
Jayanath Warasuwitana / Shamalie Fonseka
Bars immediate family from simultaneously serving on the GC or any GC-appointed committee.
74 for · 15 against
Passed — contested
2 · GC meeting transparency
Jayanath Warasuwitana / Shamalie Fonseka
Minutes finalised within 14 days, circulated within 30; must record decisions, authorisations, objections.
65 for · 21 against
Passed — contested
3 · Eligibility criteria for office
Romesh Elapatha / Tilak Conrad
President/VP: 10+ years' membership. GenSec: 5+. Other GC: 3+. Additional Trustees: 10+.
90 for · 0 against
Passed — no opposition
4 · Term limits (GC & Additional Trustees)
Romesh Elapatha / Tilak Conrad
Max 2 consecutive years same office; max 4 total; then a mandatory 2-year hiatus.
71 for · 0 against
Passed unanimously
5 · Membership admissions transparency
Soraya Cader / Romesh Elapatha
New applicant details posted within 2 weeks; admitted members displayed and read out at the next AGM.
Passed unanimously (vote count not recorded)
6 · Replace Rule 21 — Suspension
Tilak Conrad / Romesh Elapatha
Full due-process overhaul: notice of charges, 5-person Disciplinary Committee, right of defence, 1-month timeframe.
78 for · 0 against
Passed — no opposition
7 · Replace Rule 22 — Expulsion
Tilak Conrad / Romesh Elapatha
Mirrors Resolution 6's due-process structure, applied to expulsion.
83 for · 0 against
Passed unanimously
Live tension
Resolutions 6 & 7 passed with no opposition at the very meeting where members argued on the floor the Club had just breached this kind of due process, in the Selvanayagam suspension. The Chair maintained no breach occurred. Unresolved — see Governance Flag 34.

Brian Selvanayagam expelled from GC and suspended from membership. Members argue due process (Rules 21/22) wasn't followed; Chair maintains it was. Contested, unresolved.

Reinstated incumbent VP and GenSec still lost their seats despite the override. Reinstated AT nominees won theirs. NC-approved nominee Butani did not get elected.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.16-17
37Member Conduct Complaint
Complaint alleges Kishin Butani ran a WhatsApp group circulating defamatory information about nominees. Outcome not confirmed in documents reviewed.
Soraya Cader & Shamalie Fonseka email, 19 Mar 2026
38Unauthorised Incorporation
Unauthorised "Colombo Swimming Club" entity registered without approval, later struck off. Mohan Wijesinghe says he personally surfaced this, attributing the error to a prior GC.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.2, 5-6
39Constitutional Consolidation
Constitution never properly consolidated across years — a root cause of the Article 13A ambiguity itself. New Independent Constitutional Committee proposed, ~6-month timeline via SGM.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.8-9
40Resolutions Passed
Seven member resolutions passed — family conflict-of-interest, minutes transparency, eligibility, term limits, admissions transparency, and due-process rewrites of Rules 21 & 22.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.13-15
41Amendments Deferred
Six amendments to the resolutions above were never voted on — deferred to the new Constitutional Committee. One member formally objected to future amendment of adopted resolutions.
AGM Minutes, 28 Mar 2026, p.15
